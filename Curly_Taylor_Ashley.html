Known to History as Curly the Crow Scout

The enemy of my enemy

As a child the future Chief Plenty Coups had a vision that told the Crow that they as a tribe must support the rising American power or face destruction

Custer's Scout

Curly represented the Crow tribe in their alliance with the US Army. Custer released all Crow scouts before the Battle of the Little Bighorn. It was Curly who brought news of the 7th Cavalry's defeat to US Forces

Quote from: Genealogytrails.com

Howard Boggess, a descendant of one of Custer's scouts, lived in Hardin, about 20 miles north of the battlefield. His great-grandmother was Woman Who Walked into the Clouds. Her brother was Curly, the scout who according to some accounts survived by hiding in tall grass as men died around him. "He lived with the battle the rest of his life," Boggess says. "My mother said that every morning, Curly would get up early and ride the battlefield and sing chants for the people who died."
